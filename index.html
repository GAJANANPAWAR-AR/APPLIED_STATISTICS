🎲 Chapter 2 – Probabilistic Modeling of Uncertainty

Foundations: Sample space S (robot states: Idle,Running,Fault). Axioms: $P(S)=1$, $0\le P(E)\le1$, additivity. Addition rule: $P(A\cup B)=P(A)+P(B)-P(A\cap B)$. Multiplication: $P(A\cap B)=P(A)P(B)$ for independent events.
Law of Total Probability: $P(B)=\sum P(A_i)P(B|A_i)$.
📍 Automation example: 3 lines → Defect rate = $0.3×0.02 + 0.45×0.03 + 0.25×0.04 = 0.0295$ (2.95%)

🧠 Bayesian Reasoning – Fault Diagnosis

Prior $P(F)=0.02$, Sensor $P(Alarm|F)=0.98$, $P(Alarm|\neg F)=0.04$. $$P(Alarm)=0.02·0.98+0.98·0.04=0.0588$$ Posterior $P(F|Alarm)=\frac{0.0196}{0.0588}=0.3333$ (33.3%). Interpretation: Even with good sensor, low prior yields moderate posterior, need redundant sensors.

Conditional Probability: $P(A|B)=P(A\cap B)/P(B)$. Bayes' theorem: $$P(A_i|B)=\frac{P(A_i)P(B|A_i)}{\sum P(A_j)P(B|A_j)}$$. All formulas automated decision-making.

📐 Chapter 4 – Statistical Inference for Automation Decisions

Z-interval (σ known): $n=36,\bar{x}=12.5,\sigma=1.8$ → 95% CI $[11.912,13.088]$
t-interval (σ unknown): $n=12,\bar{x}=2.08,s=0.187$ → 95% CI $[1.961,2.199]$ mm
CI for proportion: 4/100 defects → 95% CI $[0.0016, 0.0784]$

📌 Hypothesis Testing

t-test (H₀: μ=2.0) → t=1.481, df=11, t_crit=2.201 → fail to reject H₀ (p≈0.166). No significant bias.
Z-test: known σ=0.2, n=30, $\bar{x}=2.09$, Z=2.46 > 1.96 → reject H₀.
⚠️ Type I error (false alarm), Type II error (missed fault) crucial in automation.

🎯 CI comparison: Known σ → narrower interval (11.91,13.09) vs unknown σ t-interval (1.96,2.20) shows uncertainty from estimating σ.
